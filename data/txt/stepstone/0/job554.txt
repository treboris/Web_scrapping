Your tasks
Future products from Bosch are connected and learn during operation. Especially for dynamic systems interacting with each other new challenges arise for developing and safeguarding such functionalities. We focus on developing new approaches at the interface between control theory and computer science to ensure efficient controls for heating systems in buildings.
As part of  our team, you will analyze, model as well as simulate dynamic  systems and develop safe approaches for solving complex learning such as control engineering problems.
You will design data- and model-based methods as well as test their robustness. Furthermore, you apply these methods to constrained linear, nonlinear, and distributed systems with parameter and structural uncertainties.
Last but not least, you maintain close contact with the customer, take into account product-specific requirements, prepare solution approaches and minimum viable software products in a target-group-oriented manner.
Your profile
Education: excellent PhD in the field of Computer Science, Electrical Engineering, Cybernetics or Mechanical Engineering
Experience and Knowledge: sound knowledge of model-based linear and nonlinear control theory, experience with controls of heating systems, identification methods based on physical and data-based models, practiced use of modern software tools and interest in software engineering methods
Personality: willing to take initiative and responsibility
Working Practice: methodical, structured, solution-oriented and quality conscious
Enthusiasm: for technical challenges, affinity for developing solutions in interdisciplinary, diverse teams and implementing them up to the functional model
Languages: very good in English
