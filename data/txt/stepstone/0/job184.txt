Olympus is passionate about creating customer-driven solutions for the medical, life sciences and industrial equipment industries. For more than 100 years, Olympus has focused on making people’s lives healthier, safer and more fulfilling by helping to detect, prevent and treat disease, furthering scientific research and ensuring public safety. Olympus is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, with more than 30,000 employees worldwide in nearly 40 countries. Olympus Europa, headquartered in Hamburg, Germany, serves the EMEA (Europe, Middle East & Africa) region and employs more than 7,800 people in 21 countries. Apply now!
Your Responsibilities
Execute security architecture reviews for data protection and data
Demonstrate experience in security solution design using existing and emerging technologies to achieve enterprise solutions
Conduct IT security reviews for proposed new applications or solution requests from business to ensure they adhere to Olympus data protection and privacy guidelines and compliance standards
Identify, define and communicate security requirements that enable adherence to data privacy standards, policies and procedures
Review and advise on defined security and privacy architecture roadmap, guidelines and requirements
Liaise with Olympus Security Architecture Global Lead to coordinate and ensure security and privacy reviews of new applications proposed by Business adhere to compliance standards
Assist application and MSP team to help remediate challenges in implementing defined architecture due to local compliance and regulatory requirements
Review and advise on audit plans and results of regional specific security architecture requirements
Liaise with architects from other domains and regions
Review current system security measures and recommend enhancements, advise on plans and results of regular system tests and ensure continuous monitoring of network security
Lead, motivate, develop and appraise team members
Your Qualifications
Bachelor’s degree in Information Technology/Security, Computer Science or other related field
CISSP-ISSAP, GPEN, GICSP, GRID, SABSA, CNDA, CRTSA, GDSA desirable
Several years of relevant work experience in corporate security architecture environments
Deep understanding of information security topics and experience working in a position requiring a deep knowledge of data protection and privacy regulations
Thorough knowledge of security architecture frameworks and cybersecurity frameworks (ABSA, TOGAF, ISO 27001/27002, COBIT, BCM, IT Risk Management, ISA-TR99.00.01-2001, ISA-TR99.00.02-2004, FIPS Pub 1999, NIST 800 (37, 82, 53, 53A), NISTIR 7628, DHS Presidential Directive 7, NERC CIP v6)
Several years’ experience leading teams (preferably across various nations) and ideally liaising between different teams
