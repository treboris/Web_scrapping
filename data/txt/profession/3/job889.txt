MOL is looking for a
Reservoir Engineer
specializing in reservoir development who has a broad understanding of reservoir engineering tools and capable of operating as a front-end among subsurface disciplines. Drives the subsurface related tasks,  identify and delivers field development proposals (infill drilling, EOR/EGR, production optimization), compile field development plans.
Carry out reservoir engineering studies using appropriate, state-of-the-art techniques including reservoir simulation, material balance, decline curve analysis, well test analysis, PVT and SCAL data analysis.Have at least 5 years of practical experience in reservoir engineering, preferably field development projectsHave active knowledge and user experience with reservoir engineering software as OFM, Eclipse, PVT Sim, Petex Mbal, ProsperAre capable of working independently, able to deliver reservoir studies, production history analysis, production and/or pressure forecasts for active and idle fields/reservoirs/wellsSupport production optimization efforts (Well-workover, acidizing, hydraulic fracturing, artificial lift system, tie-in, surface and choke optimization etc.)Are able to coordinate technical work related to field development projects and tasksSupport exploration projects with production forecastingAre confident communication level in English both in oral and written formAre confident user of MS Office (Outlook, Powerpoint, Excel, Word)You must be a team player
Experience/knowledge in well completions/well stimulation/surface facilities/artificial lift systems/EOR-EGR methodsExperience in inert gas utilizationExperience with HC-fields in HungaryExperience with Petex GAP or similarWilling to drive/coordinate small study teams/projects
Competitive reward package (a market competitive base salary, annual bonus based on individual and corporate performance, cafeteria, iPhone, health package)Good team spirit and friendly colleaguesExciting development opportunities Please consider the deadline of your application is 16th of April 2023.
