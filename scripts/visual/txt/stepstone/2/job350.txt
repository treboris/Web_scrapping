Olympus is passionate about creating customer-driven solutions for the medical, life sciences and industrial equipment industries. For more than 100 years, Olympus has focused on making people’s lives healthier, safer and more fulfilling by helping to detect, prevent and treat disease, furthering scientific research and ensuring public safety. Olympus is headquartered in Tokyo, Japan, with more than 30,000 employees worldwide in nearly 40 countries. Olympus Europa, headquartered in Hamburg, Germany, serves the EMEA (Europe, Middle East & Africa) region and employs more than 7,800 people in 21 countries. Apply now!
Your Responsibilities
Own, draft and maintain the IT security policy framework with regard to data protection, including the underpinning standards and guidelines
Oversee the adherence to regulatory requirements and current legislation and assure compliance in how they are reflected in internal policies and standards
Collect data protection concerns from stakeholders and collaborate with IT Security and Privacy to drive data protection controls and mechanisms
Oversee and manage data protection activities and projects to ensure timely completion and quality in alignment with regulatory requirements
Ensure and perform assessments of data protection processes and services on a regular basis in terms of compliance with the organization’s policies and standards
Closely collaborate on outstanding regulatory issues by liaising with risk management
Maintain and gather expertise on state-of-the-art cyber security as well as data protection trends and technologies to be leveraged for the mitigation of future security exposures
Raise cyber awareness among employees within the organization
Lead, motivate, develop and appraise team members so that their individual and collective performance is of the required standard and meets the current and future needs of the business
Your Qualifications
Bachelor’s degree or higher in Cyber Security, Information Security, Engineering, Computer Science, Information Technology, Information Management, Information Sciences, Business Administration or equivalent and ideally related experience
Multiple years of experience working with IT security policy frameworks, standards and guidelines
Several years of experience with data protection and IT privacy (e.g., GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA)
Strong expertise in risk management and regulatory requirements
Several years of experience leading teams, preferably across various nations
Demonstrable critical thinking
Detail-orientated way of working
